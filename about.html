Project Lrn is meant to be platform that will assist in the adaptation of current textbook content into interactive "game"-like modules that students will readily have access to on any smartphone, tablet, or computer. This project isn't about creating new educational content, but rather utilizing pre-existing content from reputable textbook publishers in conjunction with extremely popular mobile gaming platforms.

Project Lrn aims to partner these textbooks publishers with the game developers and provide a checklist of stringent guidelines to the educational "games" that would then be distributed on the Lrn Platform when completed (akin to how current app stores distributed applications). This consistent experience across each individual "game" (i.e. "textbook") and platform (i.e. smartphones, tablets, and laptops) would therefore make learning subjects like finance or statistics as enjoyable as any bird-based tapping game currently available for consumption.

At the end of the day, Project Lrn isn't just about making education more enjoyable; it's about putting the capability of learning in the hands of individuals who wouldn't otherwise have taken the opportunity to learn about that subject matter at that particular point in time.

About Project Lrn

Project Lrn is an initiative that was started by one individual: Gary Sahota, a full time MBA student at the Rady School of Management at the University of California, San Diego.
